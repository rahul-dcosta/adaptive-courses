## Content Strategy

### Content Pillars
1. **Speed Learning** (how to learn fast, strategies, science)
2. **Use Cases** (job interviews, client meetings, presentations, travel)
3. **AI in Education** (personalized learning, course generation, future of education)
4. **Professional Development** (career growth, skill stacking, staying relevant)

### SEO Tactics

**On-Page:**
- Title tags: Include primary keyword + benefit
- Meta descriptions: Call out urgency/speed
- H1: Match search intent exactly
- H2s: Use long-tail variations
- First 100 words: Include primary keyword 2-3 times naturally
- Internal linking: Connect related use cases
- Schema markup: Course, FAQ, Article

**Content Format:**
- **Problem-first:** Start with pain point reader googled
- **Story hooks:** "I had a factory visit in 8 hours..."
- **Actionable:** Include takeaways even if they don't buy
- **CTA:** Clear next step ("Generate your course now")

**Link Building:**
- Guest posts on indie hacker blogs
- Product Hunt launch post
- Reddit AMAs on r/entrepreneur, r/productivity
- Twitter threads documenting the build
- Indie Hackers case study

## Keyword Research Findings

### What People Search Before Events

**Job Interviews:**
- "prepare for [role] interview overnight"
- "learn [skill] before interview"
- "crash course for interview prep"

**Client Meetings:**
- "understand [industry] quickly"
- "learn about [company] before meeting"
- "client meeting preparation checklist"

**Presentations:**
- "how to present on [topic] with no experience"
- "quick research for presentation"
- "become expert in [topic] fast"

**Career Development:**
- "learn new skill fast for promotion"
- "upskill quickly for new role"
- "industry knowledge crash course"

### Search Volume Insights (Estimated)
- "crash course [topic]": 10K-50K/month (varies by topic)
- "learn [topic] fast": 5K-20K/month
- "prepare for interview": 50K+/month
- "AI course generator": 1K-5K/month (growing)

### Competition Analysis
- **Low competition:** Specific use case combinations ("interview prep overnight", "client meeting preparation course")
- **Medium competition:** General fast learning ("learn quickly", "crash course", "learn [topic] fast")
- **High competition:** Broad educational terms ("online course", "learning platform")

**Strategy:** Target medium-competition keywords with volume (interview prep, client meetings, presentations) rather than ultra-niche terms. Build authority through content, then compete for broader terms.

## Conversion Optimization

### Landing Page Structure
1. **Hero:** "Generate a crash course on any topic in 60 seconds"
2. **Problem:** "You have a meeting/interview/presentation tomorrow and need to sound intelligent"
3. **Solution:** "AI-generated, personalized courses that teach you exactly what you need"
4. **Social proof:** Testimonials, example courses
5. **How it works:** 3 steps (topic → generate → learn)
6. **Pricing:** Clear, simple
7. **CTA:** "Generate Your Course Now"

### A/B Test Ideas
- Headline: "Learn [Topic] in 1 Hour" vs. "Generate a Crash Course in 60 Seconds"
- CTA: "Generate Course" vs. "Start Learning" vs. "Get Prepared"
- Pricing: $5 vs. $7 vs. $10
- Positioning: "AI Course Generator" vs. "Crash Course Platform" vs. "Just-in-Time Learning"
